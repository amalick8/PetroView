# PetroView

**AI-driven energy analytics platform analyzing global oil supply, price dynamics, and supply shocks using Python and statistical modeling.**

---

## Overview

PetroView is a data science project that analyzes global oil supply and oil price dynamics using Python, statistical analysis, and interactive visualizations.

The project explores how geopolitical events, production changes, and supply disruptions influence global energy markets. By analyzing production data and oil price trends, PetroView demonstrates how data science techniques can be applied to real-world economic and geopolitical problems.

---

## Why This Matters

Oil is one of the most strategically important commodities in the global economy. It powers transportation, manufacturing, energy production, and international trade.

Because of this, geopolitical conflicts involving major oil-producing regions can quickly impact global markets.

Tensions involving the **United States, Israel, and Iran** highlight how instability in the Middle East can affect global oil supply. Iran sits near the **Strait of Hormuz**, a critical shipping route through which roughly **20% of the world's oil supply passes**.

If conflict disrupts oil production facilities or shipping routes in this region, global oil prices can spike rapidly due to reduced supply and increased market uncertainty.

These supply disruptions can lead to:

- rising energy prices
- global inflation pressure
- increased transportation costs
- economic instability in oil-dependent economies

Understanding these relationships through data analysis is important for economists, policymakers, and energy analysts.

## What PetroView Analyzes

PetroView explores several key aspects of global energy markets:

### Global Oil Production
Visualization of oil production levels across major producing countries.

### Oil Price Trends
Time-series analysis of crude oil prices to understand historical market behavior.

### Supply vs Price Relationships
Analysis of how changes in oil supply correlate with oil price movements.

### Supply Shock Detection
Identification of sudden drops in oil production that may indicate geopolitical disruptions or market shocks.
